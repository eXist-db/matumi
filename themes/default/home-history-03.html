Optical Character Recognition or Manual Typing

Scanned pages are mere image files. To draw out the text, either an OCR software (Optical Character Recognition) or manual typing is employed. Automatic recognition is hardly possible for many of our texts, especially Asian texts with complex or outdated characters, sometimes even handwritten or printed in low quality.

Mainly for Chinese and Japanese texts, we send our digital files to a company located in Beijing, where each character is manually typed. In addition to the character digitization, these companies do a first tagging of headings, figures, paragraphs, highlightings, old character variants, etc. However, the texts need further processing with the use of scripts. This is done in a standardized way in cooperation with the Max-Planck-Institute for the History of Science, Berlin.
